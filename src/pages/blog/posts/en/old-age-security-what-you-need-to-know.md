# Old Age Security (OAS): What You Need to Know

*Updated March 1, 2026 | Reading time: 9 minutes*

**OAS is your right — no need to have worked, just lived in Canada. But the OAS clawback is one of the most costly retirement mistakes. Here's everything you need to know to protect your OAS in 2026.**

---

## What is Old Age Security?

### A Pension for All Canadians

OAS is a monthly payment that the Government of Canada sends you when you turn 65. It's your right. You've earned it by living here.

**2026 Reference Amounts:**

| Age | Approximate Monthly | Approximate Annual |
|-----|--------------------|--------------------|
| 65 to 74 | ~$727 | ~$8,724 |
| 75 and over | ~$800 (+10% automatic) | ~$9,600 |

The government adjusts these amounts **4 times per year** to follow the cost of living. If inflation rises, your OAS rises too.

### The Automatic Boost at 75

When you turn 75, your OAS automatically increases by **10%** — no application required. This is a permanent enhancement that is indexed for the rest of your life.

---

## Who is Entitled to OAS?

### The 3 Simple Conditions

1. **Be 65 or older**
2. **Be a Canadian citizen or legal resident**
3. **Have lived in Canada at least 10 years since age 18**

That's it! No need to have worked, no need to have contributed.

### How Much Will You Receive?

The amount depends on how many years you've lived in Canada:
- **40 years or more in Canada:** full amount
- **Less than 40 years:** your amount is reduced proportionally

**Example:** If you've lived 30 years in Canada, you receive 30/40 = 75% of the maximum amount.

---

## ⚠️ CRITICAL: The OAS Clawback (2026 Update)

### When the Government Takes Back Your Money

If your income is too high, the government recovers part or all of your OAS. This is the "clawback."

**2026 Thresholds:**

- **Clawback begins at: $95,323** net income
- **Rate: 15¢ per dollar** above the threshold
- **Fully clawed back at approximately** ~$153,000

> ⚠️ **Warning**: The threshold was updated in 2026. Some older guides still show amounts like $86,912 or $90,997 — these figures are no longer current.

**Concrete example 2026:**
- Your income: $100,000
- Amount above threshold: $100,000 − $95,323 = **$4,677**
- Clawback: $4,677 × 15% = **$701/year**

You lose $701 of OAS for that year.

**High income example:**
- Income of $110,000
- Amount above threshold: $14,677
- Clawback: $14,677 × 15% = **$2,202/year** — nearly all of your OAS!

## Strategies to Protect Your OAS

### 1. Monitor Your RRIF Withdrawals

Your RRIF withdrawals count fully as income. The more you withdraw, the more you risk exceeding $95,323.

**Tip:** Withdraw only the mandatory minimum if your other income is already high. And if you can, convert part of your RRSP to TFSA during lower-income years.

### 2. Use Your TFSA Wisely

Money you withdraw from your TFSA does **not** count as income. It's your best weapon against the clawback.

**Strategy:** If your RRIF withdrawals risk pushing you above $95,323, use your TFSA to cover extra expenses.

### 3. The RRSP Meltdown Strategy (Between Ages 60 and 70)

This is the most powerful strategy for protecting your OAS long-term.

**Principle:** Between ages 60 and 70, before your government pensions stack up, your taxable income is often in lower brackets. This is the ideal time to gradually withdraw from your RRSP (~$12,000–$20,000/year).

**Result at age 72:** Your RRIF is smaller → mandatory minimum withdrawals are smaller → total income stays below the $95,323 OAS clawback threshold.

> For the complete strategy: [The Smart Order to Withdraw Your Money in Retirement](/posts/en/smart-withdrawal-order)

### 4. Delay Your OAS if You Can Afford It

You can wait until age 70 to start your OAS. Each month of waiting after 65 increases your pension by **0.6%**.

**If you wait 5 years (until 70): +36% for life!**
- $727 → $988/month (for life, indexed)

This strategy is particularly advantageous if your income between 65 and 70 is already high (clawback risk). You take a boosted OAS at 70 when your other taxable income can be better managed.

### 5. Plan Your Capital Gains Realizations

Capital gains count as taxable income. Spread your investment sales over several years to avoid a high-income year that triggers the clawback.

## The Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)

### Extra Money for Low Incomes

If your income is low, you might also receive GIS in addition to your OAS.

**Approximate 2026 Eligibility Thresholds:**

| Situation | Maximum Annual Income (excl. OAS) |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| Single | ~$22,800 |
| Couple (both receive OAS) | ~$29,808 |
| Couple (only one receives OAS) | ~$54,500 |

**Approximate 2026 Maximum Amounts:**
- Single person: **~$1,100/month** (tax-free!)
- Couple (each): **~$660/month** (tax-free!)

> For full details: [Guaranteed Income Supplement: Are You Eligible?](/posts/en/gis-eligibility-are-you-eligible)

## The Allowance and Allowance for the Survivor (Ages 60–64)

If you're between 60 and 64 and your spouse receives OAS, you might be entitled to the **Allowance**.

If you're widowed between 60 and 64, you might be entitled to the **Allowance for the Survivor**.

**Approximate 2026 Amounts:**
- Allowance: ~$1,430/month
- Allowance for the Survivor: ~$1,700/month

## How to Apply?

**For OAS:**
1. Apply **6 months before** you turn 65
2. Online through My Service Canada Account, or by phone
3. Documents needed: birth certificate, proof of residence

**Automatic enrollment:** If you already receive CPP or QPP, Service Canada might automatically enroll you for OAS. But don't assume this — confirm with Service Canada directly.

---

## Tax Planning with OAS

### OAS is Taxable

Unlike GIS, your OAS pension is **taxable**. It adds to your other income.

### You Cannot Split Your OAS

Unlike certain retirement income, you **cannot** split your OAS with your spouse.

### Impact on Your Other Benefits

Your OAS can affect certain provincial programs and your senior tax credits.

---

## Special Cases

### If You've Lived Abroad

Canada has agreements with more than 50 countries (France, USA, United Kingdom, Germany, etc.). Your years lived in these countries might count toward your OAS. Consult Service Canada.

### If You Live Abroad

You can generally keep your OAS if you live abroad, but special rules apply depending on your country of residence.

## The 5 Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Forgetting to apply | You miss months of benefits |
| Using the old threshold ($86,912) | Incorrect tax planning |
| Ignoring TFSA as anti-clawback tool | Hundreds to thousands $ lost per year |
| Not doing the RRSP meltdown between 60 and 70 | RRIF too large at 72 → OAS clawback |
| Forgetting to apply for GIS if income is low | Up to $1,100/month left on the table |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I receive OAS if I still work?**
A: Yes! There's no obligation to retire to receive OAS.

**Q: Will my OAS increase with inflation?**
A: Yes, the government adjusts it 4 times per year based on the CPI.

**Q: What happens if I move provinces?**
A: Nothing! OAS is a federal program, regardless of where you live in Canada.

**Q: Is OAS indexed for life?**
A: Yes — including the 10% bonus at 75.

---

## Conclusion

Old Age Security is your safety net for retirement. But to benefit from it fully, you need to **protect** it from the clawback and **coordinate** it with your other income.

**Key Points for 2026:**
- Apply 6 months before you turn 65
- 2026 clawback threshold: **$95,323** (not $86,912!)
- TFSA is your best weapon against the clawback
- RRSP meltdown between 60 and 70 protects your OAS at 72
- The 10% bonus at 75 is automatic — no action required
